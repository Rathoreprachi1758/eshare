  1. Use of Cookies and Similar Technologies

  1. The platform uses cookies and similar technologies, such as pixels, to provide, improve, protect, and promote our services. For the purposes of this Policy, we will refer to all of these technologies as “cookies.” This page includes information on the what, who, and how of these technologies in connection with the Platform and services.

  1. What are cookies?

  1. A cookie is a small data file sent to your browser when you visit a website. A pixel tag ("pixel") is a small piece of code belonging to a third party that can be embedded on websites and emails. We use both our own cookies as well as third-party cookies.
  2. We also use local storage, including HTML5 local storage and browser cache. Local storage allows us to store information locally on your browser or device to improve your experience, such as remembering your settings for whether you prefer to view your folders in a list or as thumbnails.

  1. Why Does eShare.ai Use Cookies?

  1. Company uses different categories of cookies to provide, protect, improve, and promote our Platform and services. We use cookies to verify and authenticate your access to the Platform and our services and enhance your account's security.

  1. What Types of Cookies Does eShare.ai Use?

  1. Necessary Cookies. We use these by default, as they enable you to navigate our Platform and use its features. Without the required cookies, our Platform cannot function properly. Therefore, this is the only type of cookie that cannot be disabled. Please note that required cookies do not collect any information that can be used to identify you.
  2. Advertisement Cookies. These collect information about your online activity over time in order to personalize advertisements allowing us to limit how often you see our ads and measure the effectiveness of our marketing campaigns.
  3. Preferences Cookies: These cookies help us remember your preferences and settings, such as language preferences and user interface customization.
  4. Performance/Functionality Cookie. These collect information about how you use our Platform and its features and are used to enhance the performance and functionality of our Platform. If you choose to disable these cookies, certain functionality, such as video, may become unavailable, or you may be required to enter login credentials each time you visit our Platform.
  5. Analytics cookies. These gather data on how you interact with our Platform. They show us which pages are visited most frequently, the link you click, and other actions you take and help us detect any difficulties you may have while using our Platform.
  6. Third-Party Cookies. These may be placed on your device when you visit our Platform through third parties that use our services. Third parties may track you or gather data using these cookies. You can manually disable them in your browser. We may allow third-party service providers, such as analytics and advertising partners, to place cookies on your device through our Platform. These cookies may collect information about your online activities over time and across different websites. We do not have control over the content and policies of these third-party cookies. Please review their respective privacy policies for more information on their practices and how to opt out. The Company is not responsible for third-party cookies.

  1. Consent

  1. By using our Platform and services, you consent to the use of cookies and similar technologies as described in this Policy. However, you have the option to manage or disable cookies through your browser settings. Please note that disabling certain cookies may affect the functionality and performance of our Platform. By continuing to use our Platform and Services, you acknowledge that you have read and understood this Cookie Policy and consent to the use of cookies as described herein.

  1. Updates to this Policy

  1. We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or applicable laws. We encourage you to review this Policy periodically for any updates.

  1. How can I control the data collected through cookies?

  1. You can set your browser not to accept cookies, but this may limit your ability to use our services. Due to a lack of an agreed-upon standard for interpreting Do Not Track (DNT) signals, our systems currently don’t respond to DNT:1 signals from browsers visiting our Platform.
